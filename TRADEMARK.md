# Moira Trademark Policy

The Moira **source code** is licensed under the Apache License 2.0 (see `LICENSE`).
The Apache-2.0 license grants broad rights to use, modify, and redistribute the code.

The **name "Moira" and the Moira logo** are trademarks of the project's owner. They are
**NOT covered by the Apache-2.0 license**: a permissive code license does not grant
trademark rights. This policy explains how the name and logo may and may not be used.

The goal is the standard open-source balance (the model used by projects such as Rust,
Kubernetes, and GitLab): the code is free to fork and reuse, while the name and logo
stay reliable markers of the official project so users are not misled about what is
"official Moira."

## What you MAY do (no permission needed)

- Use the name "Moira" to **refer to** the project ("built with Moira", "compatible
  with Moira", "a Moira workflow", "running on Moira").
- State factually that your product, service, or content **uses or integrates** Moira.
- Use the name in **truthful, descriptive** ways (tutorials, blog posts, talks,
  comparisons).
- **Fork the code** and distribute your fork — under a **different name and branding**.
- Keep existing Moira trademarks/logos intact in **unmodified** redistributions of the
  official builds.

## What you may NOT do (requires written permission)

- **Name your fork, product, or service "Moira"** (or a confusingly similar name) or use
  the Moira logo as the identity of your offering. A fork must use its own name.
- Present a fork or modified build as the **official Moira** project, release, or image.
- Use "Moira" (or the logo) in your **company name, product name, domain name, app name,
  or social handles** in a way that suggests it is the official project or is endorsed by
  it.
- Use the Moira name or logo to **imply sponsorship, affiliation, or endorsement** that
  does not exist.
- Use the name or logo on **merchandise** for sale without permission.

## Modified versions

If you distribute a modified version of Moira, you must make clear that it is your
modified version and **not** the official Moira — including using a distinct name for the
modified distribution. Truthful "based on Moira" / "fork of Moira" descriptions are fine.

## Hosted / managed services

You may run Moira (including as a service) under the terms of the Apache-2.0 code
license. However, you may **not market that service under the "Moira" name or logo** as
if it were the official Moira service. Name your service something else; you may state it
"runs Moira" or is "powered by Moira."

## Why this matters

Because Moira is permissively licensed (Apache-2.0), the trademark is the primary way
users can tell the official project apart from forks and third-party services. Protecting
the name is what keeps "Moira" a trustworthy signal — it does not restrict your freedom to
use, modify, or host the code.

## Questions and permission requests

For permission to use the Moira name or logo beyond what this policy allows, or if you are
unsure whether a use is permitted, contact the project maintainers via the repository's
profile / the contact listed there. We are happy to work with good-faith uses.
